EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — The personal data economy is broken.

Not because the supply does not exist. Not because the demand does not exist. Because there is no legal infrastructure underneath it.

Three and a half billion people generating unowned digital wealth every day. Every corporation, government agency, and regulated industry hungry for authenticated consumer data and legal consumer agents. Neither side can reach the other.

MemoryBinder builds the infrastructure that connects them.

Not another pay-for-data platform. Every attempt to pay consumers for their data has failed. Payment without ownership is still extraction. The consumer has no legal standing and the market has no foundation.

MemoryBinder starts earlier. Before the transaction. At the property right itself.

Built by lawyers and technologists with years of experience building authenticated data and AI frameworks for enterprise. That same infrastructure applied at the consumer level for the first time.

People keep doing what they love. Sharing memories. Gifting content. Telling their story. MemoryBinder makes sure that story and the responsibility is legally theirs.

THE PROBLEM — We lost control of our own stories.

People have always kept memories. Photos in boxes. Letters in drawers. Things to hold. Things to pass on.

Then came social media. And we handed all of it over.

Now the trust is gone. The ads that know too much. The feed designed to hold attention, not serve it. The slow realisation that every photo shared, every moment posted, every connection made is being fed into AI systems nobody really consented to train.

The instinct to leave is real. But leaving means losing something. Twenty years of memories. Family history. A life's worth of moments, held on someone else's infrastructure, under someone else's terms.

So people stay. Uncomfortable. Aware. Trapped.

And it is about to get harder. Agents are coming. Personal data is no longer sitting still. It is being acted on. Transacted with. Decisions are being made with it, by systems that have no legal accountability to the person it belongs to.

The rights have always existed. They just do not travel with the asset.

Digital property needs digital legal infrastructure. Legal rights and responsibilities that move at the same pace, the same scale, and stay connected to the content they protect. Everywhere it goes.

WHY NOW? — The asset is appreciating. The risk is compounding. The gap is widening.

AI models are being trained on personal content. Agents are acting on personal data. The digital estate people have built over twenty years is no longer passive. It is active. Valuable. Being used in ways that were unimaginable when the first photo was posted.

And the legal attribution gap is growing with it. As agents proliferate, the question of who owns what, who is responsible for what an agent does with whose data, has no answer. Not because the law is silent. Because the infrastructure to connect legal rights to digital assets at speed and scale does not exist at the consumer level.

The asset is getting more complex. More valuable. More dangerous.

The moment to build the infrastructure is now. Before the gap becomes permanent.

THE INSIGHT — When rights are clear, everyone wins.

This is not a privacy problem. It is an ownership problem.

Privacy tools exist. They slow extraction. They do not create ownership.

The answer is property rights. When rights are clear, markets function. When a seller knows what they own and a buyer knows what they are acquiring, value flows cleanly to the right places.

Property law did not exist to make landowners rich. It existed to make land transactions possible. Clear title is what creates a market. Everything else follows.

When rights are clear, everyone wins. Not just as a market mechanic. As the right thing to do. And for once, those two things are exactly the same thing.

THE PRODUCT — Everything people already love doing. With ownership built in underneath.

MemoryBinder looks and feels like the sharing and gifting applications people already use. That is intentional. People share photos, stories, milestones, moments. They gift content to family. They build collections. Nothing about the behaviour changes.

What changes is what happens underneath.

Every piece of content is legally authenticated. Provenance recorded. Ownership established. A legal property right that travels with the asset. Not a platform promise. A right that holds.

Every AI agent carries a legal wrapper. Legal identity. Accountability. The governance structure that means any organisation with a responsible AI policy can allow them in. Nobody needs to understand any of this. They share. They gift. They collect. They tell their story.

What they build, organically, is a legal property portfolio. Not crypto. Not tokens. Real digital property. And because every right travels with every asset, those assets can move. Licensed. Traded. Gifted. Into a marketplace where authenticated personal property finds its value.

For the first time, the people who created the value participate in it.

HOW IT WORKS — Same behaviour. Radically different outcome.

A user shares a photo. MemoryBinder authenticates it as their legal property. Provenance recorded. Ownership established. The right travels with the asset.

A user gifts audio memories to a family member. The transfer is legally documented. The recipient owns it. The giver chose to give it.

A user creates an AI agent. MemoryBinder wraps it with legal identity. It now has the governance structure that enterprise and government platforms require to allow it in.

And the portfolio grows.

Every asset added increases the value of what came before. But MemoryBinder does not just store what people create. It connects their digital property to the legal events and transactions that define a life.

Birth records. Death records. Marriage certificates. Travel. Contracts. Licences. Via Smart Legal Contracts and verified API connections, real world legal events attach to the digital estate. The asset record deepens over time. Authenticated. Legally connected. Portable.

When a user chooses to license content, the terms travel with it. When they trade an asset, the transaction is recorded. When life changes, the legal record reflects it. A property portfolio that grows with a life. That can be added to, curated, and passed on.

WHO IT'S FOR — Two markets. Both massive. Both completely underserved.

Creators. They know their work has value. What they have never had is legal architecture to own it at the infrastructure level, without a label, an agent, or a corporate entity behind them. MemoryBinder gives individual creators the legal standing a studio has always had.

Custodians. The larger market. The billions of people who are not professional creators but who are the keepers of family history, community memory, cultural record. Grandparents with sixty years of photographs. Parents documenting their children growing up. Community leaders preserving local stories. Enormous digital wealth. No mechanism to own, protect, or pass it on.

Both markets share one thing. They have been generating value for platforms for two decades and received reach in return.

MemoryBinder offers them clear title.

COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE — Behaviour is easy to copy. Legal infrastructure is not.

Social platforms extract value from consumers. Privacy tools block extraction but create no ownership. Web3 attempted tokenisation but required consumers to think like traders. Pay-for-data platforms added a revenue layer on top of a broken ownership structure.

None of them built the legal infrastructure that makes ownership real. MemoryBinder sits in a different category. Not because of features. Because of foundation.

Built by lawyers and technologists who have spent years building authenticated data frameworks for enterprise. That same infrastructure applied at the consumer level for the first time.

No competitor currently operates at this architectural layer. Sharing apps can be replicated in months. Legal infrastructure cannot. The frameworks, the authentication protocols, the Smart Legal Contract layer, the agent governance structures. These take years to build and require deep legal and technical expertise to build correctly.

Every authenticated user strengthens the network. Every piece of legally verified content adds to the provenance record. Every enterprise and government relationship built on clean, registered and authenticated consumer data deepens the moat.

Legal infrastructure networks compound differently than social networks. Social networks grow by adding users. Legal infrastructure networks grow by adding authenticated provenance. Every record makes every other record more credible. The network becomes more valuable as it grows and harder to displace as it scales.

First mover in legal infrastructure is the deepest moat in technology. MemoryBinder is building it at the consumer level. Where no one has been before.

The supply side is 3.5 billion people generating unowned digital wealth every day.

The timing is the convergence of consumer awareness, a rapidly appreciating personal data estate, and enterprise and government demand that has no legitimate supply.

Eyebrow: The Trust

H1: A data trust, in writing.

What we promise to do — and refuse to do — with the property people place in our care. Read these as binding terms, not marketing copy.

Six promises

  1. Title before transaction. We do not pay people for their data. We give them legal title to it. Payment without ownership is still extraction.
  2. The right travels with the asset. If a memory leaves MemoryBinder — gifted, exported, transferred — its legal record goes with it. Nothing locked behind our walls.
  3. No model trains on you without consent. Your memories will never be silently fed into AI training pipelines. Consent is opt-in, granular, revocable, and recorded.

  5. Independent registry. MemoryBinder operates a registry that any other organisation can verify against. Not a walled garden. A piece of legal infrastructure.
  6. We are accountable to the law. Built by lawyers and technologists. Smart Legal Contracts that hold up in court. Legal liability that lands on the entity acting, not the consumer they acted on.

Eyebrow: The Registry

H1: The independent legal layer underneath the app.

The MemoryBinder Registry is the world’s first independent legal registry for personal data and consumer AI agents. Real-time. Machine-readable. Operative across organisational and national boundaries.

Aside: Live registry events · sample

Core capabilities

  • AUTHENTICATE: Register a memory, wallet, or agent and produce a legally operative record of provenance and ownership.
  • VERIFY: Any organisation can verify the registry against a given asset. Real-time, machine-readable, court-recognised.
  • TRANSFER: Move title with the asset. Gifts, sales, licences — every change of hands recorded automatically.
  • ATTACH: Link real-world legal events — contracts, certificates, agreements — to the digital record via Smart Legal Contracts.

Architecture

H2: Four layers. One legal truth.

  • Identity layer: Cryptographic identity for every consumer, every agent, every authenticated asset.
  • Provenance layer: Append-only legal record. Authentications, transfers, licences, agent actions.
  • Smart Legal Contract layer: Programmable legal agreements that perform their own obligations.
  • Verification API: An open endpoint for enterprise and government compliance to query the legal record.

Principles

  1. Independence: The registry is held by an independent Data Trust. Not the commercial entity.
  2. Interoperability: Built on open legal protocols. Other organisations can build directly against the registry.
  3. Append-only truth: Records are not edited. Updates are appended. The history of any asset is permanent.
  4. Consumer first: Every record names a person — not a platform — as the rights holder.

Draft. This is a working draft of the MemoryBinder Privacy Policy. The final version will be reviewed by independent legal counsel before launch. Treat the contents below as our intent, not a binding contract.

1. About this policy

MemoryBinder Pty Ltd ("MemoryBinder", "we", "us", "our") is the company behind the MemoryBinder app and the MemoryBinder Registry. We build legal property infrastructure for personal data, memories, and consumer AI agents.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose and protect personal information across the MemoryBinder ecosystem. It is written to be readable. Where it falls short of what the law requires, we apply the higher standard. This Policy should be read in conjunction with our Terms of Use (/legal/terms).

Questions can be directed to our Privacy Officer at privacy@memorybinder.io.

2. What we collect

We collect only what we need to deliver MemoryBinder and to keep the legal record honest.

  • Identity information — your name, contact details, and any government-issued identification you choose to provide for verified authentication.
  • Account credentials — your username, password hash, and basic profile details.
  • Content you upload — photos, audio, video, stories, and any other memory you place in your wallets. This content is your property. We do not own it. We register the legal record around it.
  • Provenance metadata — the legal record we generate when you authenticate, transfer, license or share an asset. This is the public registry layer.
  • Usage data — how you interact with the app, in aggregate, to make it better.

3. How we use what we collect

  • To operate MemoryBinder and the Registry.
  • To authenticate, verify, transfer and license assets you place under our care.
  • To meet our legal obligations.
  • To improve the product, with anonymised analytics. Never by feeding your content into model training without your explicit, granular, revocable consent.

4. The Data Trust

The MemoryBinder Registry is held in trust by an independent Data Trust, separate from the MemoryBinder commercial entity. The Trust's charter is public. The Trust has the authority to refuse uses of the Registry that contravene the Trust's charter — including by us.

5. Sharing

We do not sell personal data. We do not feed your memories into AI training. The only situations in which information leaves your control are:

  • You choose to share, gift, license, or sell an asset.
  • We are legally compelled by a court of competent jurisdiction.
  • A vendor we use to deliver the service (payment processor, cloud host) processes data on our behalf, under a written agreement that binds them to this Policy.

6. Your rights

You can access, correct, export, and delete personal information at any time. The provenance record is append-only — but you can revoke access, withdraw assets from circulation, and remove your account.

7. Security

We follow industry standard practice. We encrypt data in transit and at rest. We log access to the Registry. We will tell you, promptly, if a breach affects you.

8. Changes to this policy

We will tell you, in advance, before we change this Policy in any material way. The current version always lives at memorybinder.io/legal/privacy.

9. Contact

privacy@memorybinder.io
